Advice from Action Fraud (police)

In November 2014, the National Fraud Intelligence Bureau (NFIB) warned about fraudsters using "070" numbers, which look like UK mobile numbers but are actually premium-rate.

"070" numbers featured in over 2,600 fraud reports to Action Fraud in a single year. Known as "personal numbers", they are virtual numbers that forward incoming calls to a list of fixed or VoIP numbers chosen by the client. VoIP numbers let calls be made or received anywhere with an internet connection, which makes them much harder to trace - so fraudsters can hide behind an "070" number while masking where the calls are really going.

Although "070" numbers are legitimate, calls to them are charged at a premium rate and have long been used to scam victims:

  • Calls from a landline can cost up to 65p a minute, plus a call set-up fee of up to 51p.
  • Calls from a mobile can cost up to £1.50 a minute.
  • In extreme cases, victims have ended up with phone bills running into hundreds of pounds.

Providers of "personal numbers" include Vodafone, Magrathea and Digital Mail.

Key methods used by fraudsters

  • The fraudster texts, leaves a voicemail, or gives a missed call from the number. The victim calls back assuming it's an ordinary UK mobile, and is unwittingly connected to a premium-rate number.
  • The fraudster places a job advert on an online shopping or auction site, giving an "070" number to call for more information or to apply.
  • The fraudster contacts someone selling an item on a shopping website and sends fake PayPal emails asking for a shipping fee to be paid via Western Union before releasing payment - often giving an "070" number, so the victim runs up further charges trying to make contact.

Protection advice

  • If anyone approaches you online, or otherwise, and gives you an "070" number to call, be wary that it could be a scam - don't call it.
  • Consider reporting it to the Phone-paid Services Authority (PSA), formerly Phonepay Plus, which regulates premium-rate numbers and has statutory powers to stop mobile phone fraud and fine offenders.
  • If you, or anyone you know, has been affected by this or any other scam, report it to Action Fraud.

To report a fraud and get a police crime reference number, call Action Fraud on 0300 123 2040 or use the online fraud reporting tool at http://www.actionfraud.police.uk/

Related information

Other premium-rate numbers begin with 076 (formerly reserved for pagers), 084, 087, 090, 091 or 118.

The regulator Phonepay Plus was renamed the Phone-paid Services Authority (PSA). It can be contacted online at https://psauthority.org.uk/ or by phone: 0300 30 300 20.
