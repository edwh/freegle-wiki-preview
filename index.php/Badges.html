This page lists badges for use on websites. See also Logos for logos you can use on websites and in print.

Badges are used to:

  • promote Freegle
  • show which awards we've won
  • promote events we're taking part in

A badge is an image with a link - click the image and you're taken to the relevant site. It's a chunk of HTML you can put on your community's home page (if you're a moderator) or any other site you control. You can alter or remove the title text as you wish.

To get the badge HTML, highlight the code with your mouse, copy it (in Windows, right-click and select Copy), then paste it into your web page code or your community description. The numbers in each badge's name refer to its size in pixels - the size you see in the wiki is the size you'll see on your page.

For uploading pictures to your Freegle home page, see How to add a picture to a group's home page.

Sussex Eco Awards 2010

Adur, Bexhill, Bognor, Brighton, Burgess Hill, Burwash, Crowborough, East Grinstead, Eastbourne, Haywards Heath, Hove, Lewes, Selsey, Uckfield & Heathfield, Worthing and Tunbridge Wells (just over the border in Kent) Freegle communities were all in the running for a Sussex Eco Award, https://www.sussexecoawards.org.uk/ against some stiff competition.
