We support each other across Freegle. Every volunteer on an affiliated group is valued equally, but we all have different skills and experience, so we've built systems and procedures to make it easier to find help.

Contents

  • 1 General everyday questions
  • 2 One-to-one help
  • 3 Friendly contact
  • 4 Mediation and appeals

General everyday questions

For queries about the everyday running of your community, ask on Central Groups or the Working Groups. A wide range of people there are happy to share their experience and advice.

One-to-one help

If you're a new group and would like a more personal approach to help you through the early days, the Mentor Team is there to help - see Mentor Team Remit. Just email mentors@ilovefreegle.org.

Friendly contact

If you'd like to speak to someone who can point you in the right direction without posting on a large group, the Mailbox team at info@ilovefreegle.org can help. Freegle Mailbox Guidelines explains the parameters they, and other Freegle mailboxes, work within.

Mediation and appeals

Sometimes issues arise that need a formal, confidential route, so we offer a Complaints Procedure.

This is a more formal way of resolving disputes between parties within Freegle. We work collaboratively, so informal support comes first - talking things over with fellow volunteers (or a long-suffering spouse or partner), or raising issues on the Freegle central or working groups. But sometimes issues escalate and need third-party help, and that's where the Complaints Procedure comes in.

The Complaints Procedure isn't something to use lightly, but it's there so that everyone gets a fair hearing.
