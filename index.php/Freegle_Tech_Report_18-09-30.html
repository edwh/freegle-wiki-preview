General

Yexit has finally happened, some groups left it until the last minute which means that they had to do without some of recommended number of Admins and processes to make sure their members are aware of what is going on. To date we have 18 groups which have not moved, 2 of which are Yahoo only 3 are Yahoo and Trash nothing and 13 which were using Direct but will now be disconnected. They have one month left to see if they change their minds after which new Native groups may be set up in their place.

Direct.

Ongoing discussion on how to sort out the bad members from the good. Some feel more help in guiding members by suggesting other info they can give when posting or requesting may be better than using clever techniques to determine how good a Freegler someone might be based on their replies and behaviour.
Others do not want to add stuff and complicate it for the majority who use the present system just fine and still others think we should have no interference at all in members transaction.
Sending posts in by email. A moderator new to Direct asked us about attaching photos when posting to Freegle Direct by email. It was tested and does appear to work.
Duplicate posts on My Posts. Doubling up of Posts once with of the ‘My Posts’ tab have been reported by three moderators. One copy is a draft version and does not have the Offer/Wanted in front or the postcode suffix. Edward believes this is fixed; let him know if not.

SMS Alerts

We used up our free quota and now we have to decide whether to continue them. We send at most one a day to members so we don’t pester them. The click rate is very high. That suggests that people who have this turned on are using it as their primary way of being notified of new messages. If they didn't like it, they wouldn't click.
10.5% of people provide one, which is 5349 since we started doing this. This proportion is pretty constant over time. We have a lot of people who have received more than 20 SMSs. That suggests that people don't get fed up with it; if they did they'd turn it off.
We're fairly steady at roughly 300 per day, with a slow upward trend these cost us $0.03 per SMS, so that's about $9/day, $279 per month, or £215 per month. I think that is a cost we can cope with, and those people who use it seem to find it useful but I'll take that to the Board to decide.
Edward Platform Hat

Mobile App

The new push notification method being used by the ModTools and Freegle apps is giving an insight into how many active users we have, assuming that most if not all users, have push notifications on.
For ModTools, there are a steady 50+ Android and 50+ iOS users - or user subscription tokens to be precise.
The count of Freegle app user tokens has steadily been increasing since this new method was introduced at the end of August. Currently there are nearly 800 Android user tokens and nearly 700 iOS user tokens, currently both going up by about 100 per day. I thought that there might be significantly more Android users, but not so far.
Google keeps track of the number of active installs: currently 10,287 for Freegle and 112 for ModTools. So: a lot of people have installed the app previously but either haven't upgraded or haven't run it since the new version has been released.
The new method also removes the notification token when we cannot send to the user, ie if they've removed the app - though many brief users won't get any notifications (as it stands) so we won't know if they've uninstalled the app. We could send everyone a notification of say a newsletter - this would remove the tokens for everyone who had uninstalled the app.
As stated previously, we will have to turn off the old Android notification method at some point. That service is being stopped next year, but we should probably switch it off before then. We probably could send a notification to all the users on the old method to say "please upgrade the app".
I'll almost certainly be doing new releases shortly to reduce the calls to Google for maps which are now costing us. Usage of the website is far greater; so Ed has stopped/reduced map requests there already.

PS The new notification method is Google's Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) which is free, currently at least:https://firebase.google.com/pricing/

Chris

Q. Why is chat on Modtools always in such a strange order, with messages from days ago all mixed up with messages from today?
Those not marked as seen are at the top. A reply which has been received to a post sent a while ago will take the whole conversation with that person back to the top.

Native.

I checked her settings on Yahoo and she was set to receive special notices - which is likely to mean that she set herself to " Surely if someone asked not to receive mail from us then that setting should have carried over to FD?< I am a bit concerned about those unsolicited digests... we could find ourselves at the receiving end of spam complaints.I am surprised nobody else has flagged this up - unless it's a new bug?
A It is not a bug but it is known that when groups switch to Native, these things happen. For instance if they have just been joined to our database the default is daily digest. Any members which predate Direct and have not been active will now be transferred over. Even when you regularly synch your members data it only goes back so far.
Q. Is there a maximum length for the group description?
A Maximum length is 64K although any limits like that may result in truncation, so you can see if they have happened, but probably you won't get warned in MT.
