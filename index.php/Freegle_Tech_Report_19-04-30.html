General

Financial and CO2 benefit of freegling
For a while now we've had a page where you can search for a political area (like a council) and get stats from the Freegle communities in that area, showing weights etc. Natalie fairly recently came across a bit of work by WRAP (http://www.wrap.org.uk/content/monitoring-tools-and-resources) which has a tool which can be used to model the benefits of reuse compared to how things would otherwise be disposed of. This means we can come up with a figure for the financial benefit of reuse (£711 per metric tonne) and CO2 saved (0.51 per metric tonne). This gives us a credible (because it's WRAP) way of estimating our overall impact.
On the main /stats page we do have a paragraph about methodology; I'll add a link to the WRAP stuff in there although in our recent experience with councils,they don't really care about the methodology, though whenever they've expressed a vague interest it's been quite useful as another conversation to have with them as part of building the relationship.

Direct

Chat
Many members seem to do a thumbs down by mistake. Currently we say "do a thumbs up" instead, but I think it would be useful to allow you to do this so that we don't need to get asked. So I've made a change (not live yet) which means that if you click again on a thumbs up/down, it will cancel it.
A regular complaint is about getting very short replies to offers, like 'I'll have that', or "when can I collect". There have been people complaining about that on chitchat recently.
We could bounce short replies, and tell people that their reply is too short, and ask them to write a longer one. This would help with that problem, and would be a way to encourage people to be more polite and put more useful info into their first reply. A middle ground would be to allow the reply through but tell people that short replies don't work well, giving them the opportunity to do a follow-up reply. Or we could just leave it as it is. Views?
After discussion most mods preferred to do nothing and objected to us interfering with people's messages.

Chit Chat. (Was News)
When people introduce themselves, it goes to the person they're communicating with, and also onto chitchat. That's started some good threads of discussion on there, but one thing that happens fairly regularly is that people then say "what are you offering".I've added some info the chitchat so that you can easily see what people are offering, and click through to their profile.

Digests
contain new posts, manual reposts and autoreposts. I've sorted it so that the autoreposts appear at the bottom. We had a few people who read every digest and didn't like seeing the same posts - this way they can stop scanning when they get to the autoreposts.

Mail
We do get complaints about chat messages not getting through or going into spam. Members feel we should be able to fix it. We try but it is up to the members email provider to receive it and put it in their account. From time to time we do get certain email providers to return emails not delivered properly. There are always a fair number. Occasionally the member has made a mistake with their email address, but most of them have just been bounced. Bounces can take a few days to come back, as they retry to get them through. We get lots of emails we send out bouncing back. Some are because the email account is not used, or full, but many are because we have been blacklisted, often after a period of the member leaving us in their spam box or even putting us there.
We are getting back any Yahoo bounces at present and Christian is looking into them.

Q & A.

Q. I’ve noticed on Amazon that they support charities but Freegle is not on the list, would it help if we were?
We are not the correct sort of charity but if we use everyclick we can benefit for .05% . If you install the browser toolbar it reminds you when you're on a site which will generate funds. It adds up, especially for larger items
Q. I have a complaint from a member in Welwyn Garden City that she is not able to mark a post as taken nor can she post a new post. When I checked her membership, she doesn’t show as having posted anything at all, yet the logs showed a post approved by one of our mods. So I impersonated her and her previous offer shows as a draft, yet she has replies. There are no posts in her my posts other than this draft.
A.I think she got partway through a manual repost. Checking last night's backup, that message was on Welwyn, since the 8th, so it would probably be around now that she could repost it, and it wouldn't have autoreposted because she had replies. There's a resume button there to continue with that draft. As you say, find out what goes wrong when she tries.
Q. Do we need the function to promise?
The promise button is useful for recording bad experiences with other freeglers, but now we have thumbs up and thumbs down do we really need it? I hardly ever mark an item Promised because a failure to identify the promisee as having Taken, for whatever reason, leads to "the system" marking them as possibly unreliable, and I would rather be the judge of that. Maybe it is not working, or maybe other people are doing the same, because of the last 200 posts on my home group,, none are greyed out.
A. It's not quite as blunt as that. It will only show them as possibly unreliable if things have not worked out with them more than 25% of the time. Individuals can't judge terribly well, because they only see one transaction. So someone might be promised lots of things and then say sorry and give the same convincing excuse. If they say that to half the people they deal with then they're not reliable even if every individual person they dealt with found their explanation convincing. It doesn't even have to be deliberate, people might be lovely but just never quite get their act together. If there is a pattern of them not following through on items they get promised, then that's worth flagging up.
The nature of it is that things will only stay promised until they are marked as taken, at which point they won't show at all. That means you won't see very many. So people do use it, but I would like to find ways to get them to use it more. Could we try greying out posts when, say, 5 people have responded, instead? We do show how many people have replied, but we could do more there. A. I'll think about it. Q. If you are set to receive group message notifications, either individual, daily digest etc will these show your own messages on them? A. Yes
