Local funding opportunities might be available for your Community. As Freegle Communities are autonomous, there are no restrictions from Freegle, as a central organisation, on Communities applying for funds to help with local publicity etc as long as applications clearly show that it is a local Community application only. Please do let the Board know, however, of your intention to apply for funding in case there is a conflict of interests with other Communities or nationally.

Sharing ideas and support

Freegle Funding Group is a national group that all Freegle Community moderators and owners are welcome to join [1]. It supports Communities, working together to provide information and ideas for funding.

Before contacting your local authority, it’s probably an idea to check with Freegle's council team to see if they have had any dealings with any officials in your area – partnerships@ilovefreegle.org

Constitution and bank account?

In order for your Community to access funds, you might find that the funding body you approach expects you to have a constitution and bank account in the name of the Community. An example of Community constitutions can be found at Group Constitution, which you can choose to use as a guide or template for your Community.

A good leaflet, aimed at small charities, might be worth reading about bank accounts and the responsibility of Treasurers - [2]

Non profit

It is important to remember that all Freegle Communities have to be non-profit, so have a look at some best practice guidelines to ensure you comply with that ethos.

Information for funders

Funding Application Help gives an overview of the type of information you need to think about and provide for applications, along with Local Business Plan, an example of how you might present information about your Community.

Local funders

Some links to local organisations that help voluntary Communities find and apply for funding can be found at Local Funding Opportunities.

Freegle or Community

Please remember that local Communities should not apply for or indicate that they are applying for funds for Freegle as an organisation. Unless, of course, this has been agreed with the Board. The local aspect of applications is usually also a positive aspect for local funders.

Let others know

If Communities are fortunate to obtain funding for local use, or even if they have applied and been unsuccessful, it would be very much appreciated if a message can be posted to the Local Funding Group and Freegle Central with brief details. This will provide information and encouragement for other Communities as well as keeping a record of the different types of funding being requested and/or obtained for the Freegle network of Communities.

A brilliant example of local fundraising and publicity is Publicity in Penrith and Eden District
