A Pet Mod is a volunteer who deals with posts on a community relating to live animals.

Any post about a live animal is put on hold (this can be done with a mod edit to the subject line of a message in pending) and left for the Pet Mod to deal with.

Group guidelines

Some suggested guidelines a community might adopt for moderating pet posts:

  1. All pet posts are approved by the Pet Mod.
  2. No wanteds or offers for animals to be used as feeder food.
  3. No wanteds or offers for animals to be used for breeding.
  4. No wanted posts for any exotic animal that needs special knowledge or care to keep it healthy, unless the poster has extensive experience with that species. If in doubt, reject the post and ask the member to say more about their experience, then reconsider based on their answer.
  5. No wanted posts for illegal animals, even if the law seems unreasonable.
  6. Watch for possible "pet collectors" - people who take on more animals than they can properly care for. It's the moderators' job to contact them when rejecting a post. One sign is someone repeatedly posting wanted ads for animals, especially phrases like "I'll take any animal".
  7. Watch for someone apparently trying to "unload" a large number of pets at once. Again, the Pet Mod should contact them to find out the situation, and use good judgement in deciding whether to allow the offers.
  8. It's strongly recommended to visit the adopter's home before agreeing to the adoption.

These are common-sense rules - use your head, your heart, and good judgement, and people will be glad to work with you.

Questions for a Pet Mod

Questions a Pet Mod might ask someone offering a live animal:

  • Is the animal in good health?
  • Is the animal pregnant?
  • Has it been fully vaccinated and neutered?
  • Have other options, such as animal sanctuaries, been explored?
  • Is the animal safe? (See the Dangerous Dogs Act 1991 for the UK.)

The RSPCA's advice on unwanted pets can be useful to pass on to members offering their pets.
