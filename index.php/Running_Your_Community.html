Freegle Volunteer Wiki › 🏘️ Running Your Community

This section is for volunteers running the day-to-day life of a local community: keeping members talking to each other, putting on events, and staying connected to the wider network of Freegle volunteers. It's organised by topic rather than as one long list, so dip into whichever bits are relevant to what you're doing right now - there's no need to read it end to end.

Start here: Running Your Local Community is the long-standing handbook front page for this whole area, and ChitChat is the discussion feature most communities use day to day.

Overview

  • Running Your Local Community - the classic handbook front page for this area of the wiki, with pointers on to Basic Information, First Steps, ModTools, How To Deal With and Freegle Direct.

Local community, ChitChat and neighbourhood links

  • ChitChat - the public discussion forum built into Freegle Direct, where members chat, ask questions and get to know their community; the modern replacement for the old email Cafe groups.
  • Local Community - a short hub linking the local-community pages below, plus advertising events, charities, the police, festivals and schools.
  • Advertising local events - why event notices don't belong on the main offers/wanteds listing, and where to point members instead (Freegle Direct Events or ChitChat).
  • Community Police - how one group built a working relationship with their local neighbourhood police team to share missing-person and safety appeals.
  • Festivals - why big festival clear-outs are usually best left to specialist charities, but how you can still get Freegle a mention locally and help people avoid unnecessary purchases beforehand.
  • Schools - a real example of a Freegle give-and-take run inside a school, as inspiration for building links with schools in your own area.
  • Charities and community groups - how to handle charities and community groups joining your community, and how to check whether an organisation's charity claim stacks up.

Cafe groups (the older, email-based chat groups)

  • Cafe Groups - background on the separate Yahoo-era "Cafe" discussion groups some communities set up before ChitChat existed, and how to set one up if you still want one.
  • Cafe Guidelines - a suggested welcome-and-guidelines template for a local Cafe group.
  • Cafe Disclaimer - the standard disclaimer and safety-notice text for Cafe groups.
  • Cafe Leave - a template message for members who unsubscribe from your Cafe group.

Running events: give and takes, info stalls and banners

  • Events - a short hub linking everything below, plus insurance and Freegle Direct Events.
  • Give and Take - the full guide to planning and running a give-and-take event, including how to apply for Freegle's funding contribution towards costs.
  • Collecting Stuff - how to gather stock for an event, whether that means collecting from members yourself or storing donations at the venue beforehand.
  • Running an Info Stall - how to plan and staff a simple information stall to raise your group's local profile.
  • Banner - the cloth, canvas and PVC banners you can borrow for events, and how to request one.
  • QR Codes - generating a QR code that links straight to your Freegle homepage, for banners, flyers and posters.

Getting involved nationally

  • Getting Involved - a hub pointing to Central Groups, Working Groups, Freegle Teams, the Board, Named and Contracted Roles, and more ways to take part beyond your own community.
  • Central Groups - the Discourse-hosted Central discussion groups every owner, moderator and volunteer is encouraged to join, including the main Central group itself.
  • Freegle Cafe - the "Freegle Rock Cafe", a chit-chat group on Discourse for UK volunteers to socialise away from Central.
  • Freegle Whatsapp - the formal and informal WhatsApp groups volunteers can join to keep in touch.
  • Named Roles - the individually-appointed national volunteer roles, such as Board Secretary, Ombudsman, Returning Officer and Treasurer, and any current vacancies.

Naming and local initiatives

  • Changing community name - the simple, low-risk process for renaming your local community, for example to include "Freegle".
  • Local Group Initiatives - an example of a particularly active local community, Penrith and Eden, and their local projects.
