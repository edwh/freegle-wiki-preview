Group Assessment Team (GAT) Recruitment Process (continued)

POLL QUESTION: We should propose that the Reps endorse a change to http://wiki.ilovefreegle.org/Group_Affiliation_Team_Role_Remit_Membership:
Membership: "If GAT need additional team members, the vacancy will be advertised on Freegle UK Central Group and/or GAT will approach likely candidates. Any Freegle Volunteer can apply and also have the opportunity to log their interest at any time via the Volunteer Coordinator. GAT will record the selection process/rationale and will decide who they would like to join the team. This decision must have the support of all of the GAT members. The name(s) will be put forward to the Reps for ratification and the decision will then be final. A list of the names of suitable candidates who were not selected will be retained by GAT and Reps for consideration for future vacancies."

CHOICES AND RESULTS
- No, I disagree with this proposal, 1 votes, 4.55%
- Abstain (please explain on group), 0 votes, 0.00%
- Yes, I agree with this proposal, 21 votes, 95.45%

Freegle Direct (FD) and Member Lists

We're trying to establish what happens to membership lists if a group decides to leave Freegle Direct. (The info available to Freegle via FD doesn't include whether a member is also a mod or owner.) We ran a poll:

This poll is to establish the principle of who should be able to use the group membership list kept by Freegle Direct.
Q: Is it acceptable for Freegle centrally to use the list without permission from the group's Volunteers?
We will discuss the implications/alternatives to this whether the majority answer is yes or no. This question will be carried by a simple majority.

CHOICES AND RESULTS
- Yes, it is acceptable, 4 votes, 17.39%
- No, it isn't acceptable, 19 votes, 82.61%
- Abstain (please explain on group), 0 votes, 0.00%

Full Volunteer Polls

We've not had many of these recently, and part of that is because it's quite a lot of hassle all round to run. So we've shifted more to an approach of announcing things on Central to see if people are OK with them.

That's not a bad approach, but there are a couple of problems:

  • It means that we pay most attention to the people who are most vocal, either for or against whatever it is.
  • It means our decisions have less democratic legitimacy.

We are considering whether we could have a scheme whereby we run polls on Central.

  • If the results are close, we trigger a full-blown poll with mails to -owner etc.
  • If there is a two-thirds majority, we treat it as binding in the same way in which we would if we'd done the full procedure.

Maybe if we had that, we'd do a few more such polls, and that this would be good for Freegle's participation levels.<nr> Discussion continues...
