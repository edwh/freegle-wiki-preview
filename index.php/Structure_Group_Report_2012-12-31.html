Task 96 - Setting Up a Trading Subsidiary

Discussion originally called Trading Arm and continues from Nov. Structure Report.

Employing People

We are discussing the pros and cons of employing people to work for Freegle. The scenario we are exploring initially is a freelance web designer, media or tech person to work on Freegle Direct. The discussion covers the advantages and implications of hiring someone on a freelance basis versus having a permanent paid ‘employee’. Discussion continues...

Freegle Direct Membership Lists Policy

The current wiki entry is:

Freegle centrally will not use any group's membership list kept by Freegle Direct without permission from the group Volunteers.

Volunteers are assumed to be those who receive -owner emails for the group.

Permission would be gained by emailing the group -owner address and acted upon with
- explicit permission from one Volunteer, and
- implicit permission from other Volunteers, i.e. assume consent if no conflicting replies are received within 3 weeks (reminder sent after 2 weeks within that period).

We are running a poll to make a small amendment to this.

POLL QUESTION: We should amend the wiki entry http://wiki.ilovefreegle.org/Freegle_Direct_Membership_Lists to add the sentence: " This includes groups which have chosen to leave Freegle." (see thread starting http://uk.groups.yahoo.com/group/Freegle-Structure/message/11040 for background.)

CHOICES AND RESULTS
- Yes, 19 votes, 100%
- No, 0 votes, 0%
- Abstain (please explain on group), 0 votes, 0.00%

Standing for a Separate Elected Position

Our Returning Officer, Stuart, was a candidate in the Reps election. To avoid conflict of interest he stood down from his position as RO during the elections but didn’t resign his position. He is now resuming his position to complete his current term. We don’t currently have a policy to cover this situation so the following suggestion is under discussion.

Anyone in an elected position can stand for another separate elected position. Where there is a conflict of interest (e.g. if the person is the RO) then they must stand aside from their current role until the election is complete. Once the election is complete, if the person does not have a new role then they may resume their old role and service until the end of their original period of office. They may not have access to sensitive information during this stand-aside period; for example, a RO may not have access to voting information about an election in which they stood as a candidate. If they do have a new role, or no longer wish to resume their old role, then a new election will be called to fill that vacancy. This discussion continues...

Reps Terms Of Office

The constitution says:

7.3 Elections for Reps will be held annually.

7.4 Reps will serve a term of office of three years.

7.5 At the end of each year at least one third of Reps will stand down from office and/or come to the end of their term of office.

We've got a bit out of step now, because of people stepping down earlier than 3 years. This means we might either have a situation where we do not have a third of Reps reaching the end of their term of office, or (later) might have considerably more than a third (which might be bad for continuity).

What method should we use to get back in step? Discussion continues...
